Top 12 Breathtaking Places to Visit in Kumaon Region, Uttarakhand

Sarika ChauhanApril 30, 2024April 30, 2024

The Kumaon region of Uttarakhand is a stunning Himalayan destination that truly deserves a place on every traveler’s bucket list. Nestled amidst snow-capped peaks, pristine nature reserves, and culturally rich villages, this area offers a diverse array of experiences for all kinds of visitors. Get ready to be captivated by the best that Kumaon region has to offer!

Exploring the Best of Kumaon Region

With its dramatic landscapes, ancient spiritual sites, and opportunities for adventure, Kumaon Tour is a treasure trove waiting to be uncovered. From serene hill stations to challenging treks, this region caters to those seeking respite in nature as well as thrill-seekers alike. Immerse yourself in the local culture, sample authentic Kumaoni cuisine, and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Popular Hill Stations in Kumaon

1. Nainital – The iconic lake town is arguably the crown jewel of Kumaon. With its pristine Naini Lake surrounded by hills, the vibrant Mall Road for shopping and dining, and vantage points like Snow View offering panoramic Himalayan vistas, Nainital is a must-visit.

2. Bhimtal – A tranquil town centered around a serene lake with an island park, Bhimtal offers a respite from the crowds. Enjoy boating, nature walks, and the peaceful ambiance of this charming hill station.

3. Mukteshwar – Fondly called the ‘Gem of Kumaon’, Mukteshwar is renowned for its stunning 350km panoramic views of the Himalayan ranges. The glorious sunrises and sunsets here are truly mesmerizing.

4. Kausani – This quaint hill station is famous for its incredible sunrise views of majestic Himalayan peaks like Trishul, Nanda Devi, and Panchchuli. Kausani offers the perfect vantage point to witness nature’s grandeur.

Spiritual Destinations of Kumaon

1. Jageshwar – Housing clusters of ancient Hindu temples dating back to the 7th century AD, Jageshwar is a significant spiritual and archaeological site. Don’t miss the famous Koteshwar Mahadev Temple here.

2. Baijnath – This sacred town is home to exquisite 12th-century Shiva temples built in the typical Katyuri style of architecture. The intricate carvings and imposing shrines are a sight to behold.

3. Pindari Glacier Trek – For those seeking a spiritual connection with nature, the Pindari Glacier Trek is a rewarding experience. The trek route passes through stunning glaciers, frozen lakes, and offers breathtaking mountain vistas.

Adventure Hotspots in Kumaon

1. Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary – A paradise for nature enthusiasts, this sanctuary offers opportunities for wildlife watching, bird spotting, and peaceful nature trails amidst lush forests and meadows.

2. Camping in Munsiyari – Indulge in the ultimate adventure by camping in the remote Munsiyari region near the Indo-Tibetan border. Enjoy stunning mountain views, stargazing, and the serenity of being one with nature.

3. River Rafting in Bhakra River – Get your adrenaline pumping with an exciting whitewater rafting experience on the Bhakra River. Navigate through the rapids and revel in the thrill of this adventure sport.

Other Must-Visit Places

1. Almora – Considered the cultural capital of Kumaon, Almora is a charming town with traditional bazaars, ancient temples, and a vibrant local lifestyle. Don’t miss the iconic Lal Bazaar and Nanda Devi Temple.

2. Chitai Golu Devta Temple – This ancient temple is dedicated to the local folk deity, Golu Devta, and offers a fascinating glimpse into the region’s unique traditions and beliefs.

3. Rahikhola Village – For an authentic taste of Kumaoni culture, visit Rahikhola Village. Sample the delectable local cuisine, interact with friendly villagers, and experience the simple yet rich rural lifestyle.

How to Reach Kumaon

By Air: The nearest major airport is Pantnagar Airport, located approximately 3-4 hours away from popular Kumaon destinations like Nainital and Almora. Alternatively, you can fly to Delhi and then take a bus or hire a private vehicle to reach Kumaon.

By Rail: The major railheads closest to Kumaon are Kathgodam and Tanakpur. From these stations, you can easily find transport to your desired destination within the region.

By Road: Kumaon is well-connected by road to major cities like Delhi, Chandigarh, and Dehradun. You can either drive down or opt for comfortable bus services or private taxis.

Best Time to Visit Kumaon

While each season offers its unique charm, the post-monsoon and summer months are generally considered the best time to visit Kumaon for most activities.

Travel Tips

– Pack: Carry a mix of warm and lightweight clothing, sturdy shoes for hiking, sunscreen, and essentials like a hat, sunglasses, and a water bottle.
– Stay: Kumaon offers a range of accommodation options, from budget guesthouses and homestays to luxury resorts and camps. Choose according to your preferences and budget.
– Local Cuisine: Don’t miss out on the delectable Kumaoni cuisine, known for its unique flavors and use of locally-sourced ingredients. Try dishes like Bhatt ki Churkani, Singori, and Kafuli.
– Permits: For certain treks and restricted areas, you may need to obtain the necessary permits from local authorities. Hiring a guide is recommended for such activities.
– Guides: Engaging a local guide can greatly enhance your experience, providing valuable insights into the region’s history, culture, and customs.

Kumaon is a region that truly has something for everyone. From tranquil hill stations to adventure hotspots, spiritual sites to culturally rich villages, this Himalayan gem promises an unforgettable journey.
