]]> Why India in Monsoon is a Beauty

India in monsoon is a sight to behold. The parched lands quench their thirst, the greenery comes alive, and the air feels fresher. Monsoon transforms India’s landscape into a vibrant canvas of lush forests, blooming flowers, and flowing rivers. For those seeking a refreshing escape, weekend getaways during monsoon offer the perfect blend of adventure, serenity, and cultural experiences. Here are some of the best destinations to explore during this magical season.

Delhi, Agra, Mathura, Vrindavan

Ideal For

History buffs, culture enthusiasts, families, and spiritual seekers.

Things to Do

  • Delhi: Explore the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and Humayun’s Tomb. Take a walk in the rain-washed Lodhi Gardens and enjoy street food at Chandni Chowk.
  • Agra: Marvel at the Taj Mahal in its rain-drenched glory, visit Agra Fort, and explore the Mughal gardens.
  • Mathura and Vrindavan: Visit the Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple, take a boat ride on the Yamuna River, and experience the monsoon celebrations at the ISKCON temple.

How to Reach and Interconnectivity

Delhi, Agra, Mathura, and Vrindavan are well-connected by road and rail. The Yamuna Expressway offers a smooth drive between Delhi and Agra. Regular trains and buses run between these cities, making intercity travel convenient.

Attractions

  • Delhi: India Gate, Lotus Temple, Akshardham Temple.
  • Agra: Mehtab Bagh, Fatehpur Sikri, Itimad-ud-Daulah.
  • Mathura and Vrindavan: Banke Bihari Temple, Prem Mandir, Govardhan Hill.

Weather in Monsoon

Delhi, Agra, Mathura, and Vrindavan experience moderate to heavy rainfall, making the temperature pleasant and the surroundings lush green.

Varanasi, Ayodhya

Ideal For

Spiritual seekers, history lovers, and photographers.

Things to Do

  • Varanasi: Attend the Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat, take a boat ride on the Ganges, and explore the ancient temples and narrow lanes.
  • Ayodhya: Visit the Ram Janmabhoomi, Hanuman Garhi, and Kanak Bhawan. Enjoy the peaceful ambiance of the ghats along the Saryu River.

How to Reach and Interconnectivity

Varanasi and Ayodhya are well-connected by rail and road. Direct trains and buses are available between these cities. The nearest airport to Varanasi is Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport.

Attractions

  • Varanasi: Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Sarnath, Manikarnika Ghat.
  • Ayodhya: Ramkatha Park, Nageshwarnath Temple, Ayodhya Research Centre.

Weather in Monsoon

Both cities experience moderate rainfall, which adds a mystical charm to the ghats and temples.

]]> Jodhpur’s cultural and rich traditional legacy is reflected in the city’s historical sites, each of which has a fascinating background. Tourists with the help of the travel agents in Delhi, remember the city fondly because of its Royal blue structures, archaeological monuments, historic temples, forts, and wonderfully done palaces. These sites remain untouched by any form of uncontrolled plant growth and yet preserve their magnificent beauty as they have for ages.

The fort of Khejarla

The 400-year-old Khejarla Fort can be found in a remote area around 85 kilometers from the nearest major city. Incredible in its red sandstone, this former temple is now a hotel and a prime example of the Rajput style. The fort’s stunning scenery, latticework friezes, and exquisite Jharokas will leave visitors in awe.

Mehrangarh Fort

Built by Rao Jodha in the year 1460 A.D., Mehrangarh Fort is one of the most impressive fortifications in Rajasthan. So it should be no surprise that this is one of Jodhpur, Rajasthan’s most popular tourist destinations which you can easily travel with the help of the travel agencies in Delhi.

The fort, which stands approximately 100 feet above the city, is so well constructed that it is impossible to tell where the slope stops and the walls begin. This red sandstone palace is unrivaled in its majesty and stature, and its unusual beauty only adds to its unique appeal.

Moti Mahal

With Tour and travel in Delhi explore Moti Mahal, or Pearl Palace, it is where the royal family gather for their audiences. It is well known that the hall has glass windows and five nooks that allow the queens to listen in on the activities in the Srinagar Chowki, also known as The Royal Throne of Jodhpur.

Toorji Ka Jhalra

One of the few surviving buildings illustrating Jodhpur’s old water management systems is the Toorji Ka Jhalra, also known as the stepwell of Jodhpur. The intricately carved stepwell dates back to the 18th century and is a favorite location for tourist snaps.

Jaswant Thada

White stones adorn the building, with several paintings of Rathore monarchs. A small lake and a garden with tiers may be seen on the property. You will be awed by the beautiful buff sandstone, stunning jaalis, and intricate latticework in white marble sheets.

Among the tombs of the Rathore dynasty, there is a single tombstone honoring a peacock that is supposed to have crashed into a cremation pyre. You can book now with travel companies in India to explore Jaswant Thada today.

Rao Jodha Desert Rock Park

A variety of exotic plant species are displayed in this park, which spans 200 acres. The picturesque landscapes here make it a top tourist destination in Jodhpur. Attractive Devkund Lake attracts a wide variety of birds, both migratory and resident, as well as fascinating reptiles and stunning butterflies.
