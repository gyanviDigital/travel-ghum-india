]]> Why India in Monsoon is a Beauty

India in monsoon is a sight to behold. The parched lands quench their thirst, the greenery comes alive, and the air feels fresher. Monsoon transforms India’s landscape into a vibrant canvas of lush forests, blooming flowers, and flowing rivers. For those seeking a refreshing escape, weekend getaways during monsoon offer the perfect blend of adventure, serenity, and cultural experiences. Here are some of the best destinations to explore during this magical season.

Delhi, Agra, Mathura, Vrindavan

Ideal For

History buffs, culture enthusiasts, families, and spiritual seekers.

Things to Do

  • Delhi: Explore the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and Humayun’s Tomb. Take a walk in the rain-washed Lodhi Gardens and enjoy street food at Chandni Chowk.
  • Agra: Marvel at the Taj Mahal in its rain-drenched glory, visit Agra Fort, and explore the Mughal gardens.
  • Mathura and Vrindavan: Visit the Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple, take a boat ride on the Yamuna River, and experience the monsoon celebrations at the ISKCON temple.

How to Reach and Interconnectivity

Delhi, Agra, Mathura, and Vrindavan are well-connected by road and rail. The Yamuna Expressway offers a smooth drive between Delhi and Agra. Regular trains and buses run between these cities, making intercity travel convenient.

Attractions

  • Delhi: India Gate, Lotus Temple, Akshardham Temple.
  • Agra: Mehtab Bagh, Fatehpur Sikri, Itimad-ud-Daulah.
  • Mathura and Vrindavan: Banke Bihari Temple, Prem Mandir, Govardhan Hill.

Weather in Monsoon

Delhi, Agra, Mathura, and Vrindavan experience moderate to heavy rainfall, making the temperature pleasant and the surroundings lush green.

Varanasi, Ayodhya

Ideal For

Spiritual seekers, history lovers, and photographers.

Things to Do

  • Varanasi: Attend the Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat, take a boat ride on the Ganges, and explore the ancient temples and narrow lanes.
  • Ayodhya: Visit the Ram Janmabhoomi, Hanuman Garhi, and Kanak Bhawan. Enjoy the peaceful ambiance of the ghats along the Saryu River.

How to Reach and Interconnectivity

Varanasi and Ayodhya are well-connected by rail and road. Direct trains and buses are available between these cities. The nearest airport to Varanasi is Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport.

Attractions

  • Varanasi: Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Sarnath, Manikarnika Ghat.
  • Ayodhya: Ramkatha Park, Nageshwarnath Temple, Ayodhya Research Centre.

Weather in Monsoon

Both cities experience moderate rainfall, which adds a mystical charm to the ghats and temples.

Nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and couples.

  • Jodhpur: Explore Mehrangarh Fort, visit the Umaid Bhawan Palace, and wander through the blue streets of the old city.

How to Reach and Interconnectivity

Mount Abu, Udaipur, and Jodhpur are well-connected by road and rail. Udaipur and Jodhpur have airports with regular flights. Buses and trains frequently run between these cities.

Attractions

  • Mount Abu: Sunset Point, Achalgarh Fort, Mount Abu Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Udaipur: Jag Mandir, Fateh Sagar Lake, Bagore Ki Haveli.
  • Jodhpur: Jaswant Thada, Mandore Gardens, Rao Jodha Desert Rock Park.

Weather in Monsoon

These cities experience moderate to heavy rainfall, enhancing the beauty of lakes, forts, and palaces.

Starting from numerous lakes to magnificent monuments, you will get best places to visit in Udaipur. As there are a number of lakes present in the city; it is often called “Venice of the East”.

Best Places to Visit in Udaipur

1. Fateh Sagar Lake

This is one of the four beautiful lakes whose calm water and fascinating green beauty make it one of the best places to visit in Udaipur. Moreover, nature’s endless beauty and unmatchable serenity offer you a mesmerizing reflection of its history, culture, and traditions.

Additionally, here you can participate in a variety of events and activities such as boat rides to forget your hectic travel journey and witness the green beauty and blue water in one place.

Timing: Fateh Sagar Lake is open from 8 am in the morning till 6 pm in the evening.

Ticket: Entry is free.

2. Pichola Lake

It is named after a village known as Picholi and is surrounded by palaces, islands, temples, ghats, and diverse culinary places. In fact, this place’s diverse offerings have everything for every individual tourist.

Though the main attraction that everyone admires is the view of its beautiful sunset. Alongside, a relaxing boat ride at the exact moment of sunset along with beautifully decorated boats will make you fall in love with this Places to Visit in Udaipur.

Timing: 9 am to 6 pm every day a week.

Boat Ride timing: 10 am to 5 pm.

Ticket: No entry fee is required but boat ride charges for Grownups are 400 in the morning and 700 after sunset. While children’s ticket charges are 200 and 400 consecutively.

3. Bahubali Hills

Every one of us is familiar with Udaipur’s elegant beauty and royal heritage, but do you know behind all these the place consists of some magical natural beauty such as “Bahubali hills”.

A little bit far from the main city, this nearby hill’s viewpoint will leave you amazed with its beautiful sunset views.

Best time to visit: Monsoon is the ideal season for enjoying this beautiful city.

4. Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace

Maharana Sajjan Singh built this place located on a hilltop to watch the monsoon clouds. Beside that, this place offers a beautiful view of the whole city along with its surrounding lakes, palaces, and hills. Compactly, It is an ideal place to watch the sunset and delve into a relaxing evening in Udaipur.

Timing: opening time is 9 am and closing at 5 pm.

Ticket: For Adults 250 and for Children INR 150 per head.

5. Bagore ki Haveli

You can witness the beautiful royal Rajasthani culture and the gorgeous architectural designs on this Haveli. Bagore ki Haveli is divided into several chowks where royal Rajasthani women used to perform the iconic Ghoomar dance in the middle of it in ancient days.

In particular, you can explore its culture, huge courtyards, beautiful mirror works, tranquil fountains, and decorated jharokhas if you are an art admirer like us.

Timing: 9.30 am to 5.30 pm all days of the week.

Ticket: Indians need to pay INR 60 and children’s entry fees will be INR 30.

Additionally, Foreign entry charges are 100 INR, and for children INR 50.

Additional charges are applicable on a camera of INR 50.

6. Karni Mata Temple

Don’t forget Karni Mata temple while visiting Udaipur as it is a highly religious place and what’s more is this is an abundant place for sacred rats. Goddess Durga is worshiped here as Karni Mata who holds many mystical abilities and healing powers. Alongside, devotees can spot a huge variety of rats here and explore temple rituals while enjoying the mythological depiction.

Timing: Open from 4 am to 10 pm every day a week.

Ticket: Entry is free for entering the temple.

7. Sahelio ki bari

Your Udaipur trip would be incomplete without a visit to a charming attraction named “Sahelio ki Bari”. Located on the banks of Fateh Sagar Lake, this place used to be a spot for leisure evenings of royal maidens.

Inside its colorful flower beds, marble lawns, and beautiful fountains are worth your time and perfect for attractive Instagram pictures.

Timing: Open from 9 am in the morning till 7 pm.

Ticket: The entry fee for Indians is 10 rupees but for foreigners 50 Indian rupees.

8. City palace

This 450-year-old museum palace is another major tourist attraction of Udaipur. Moreover, its unique architecture and historic lineage offer a mesmerizing birds-eye view of the city from the top of the hill.

Timing: Open all day a week 9 am to 9 pm.

Ticket: The entry fee for adults is 400 and 150 for children.

9. Gangaur Ghat

This is a wonderful place to feel the vibe of Udaipur’s vibe, especially during sunrise and sunset. It is a beautiful place for taking delightful nature pictures and it is also popular as a pre-wedding shoot destination for couples.

Timing: Every day a week from 12.00 am to 12.00 pm.

Ticket: No entry fee required.

10. Jagmandir Palace

This is a beautiful island palace where you can enjoy a relaxing boat ride from the City palace amidst the lake Pichola. Above all, this island palace offers a great sunset view for its visitors. This three-story palace also has a cafe where you can have authentic Rajasthani cuisine.

Timing: Open every day a week from 10:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.

Ticket: There is no entry free here but the boat rides cost 450 per person.

11. Ambrai Ghat

This Ghat offers one of the best views of the surrounding lakes which is a popular destination for tourists to sit by the water and enjoy the beautiful views, especially during sunset. Additionally, the Ambari ghat is also a great place for experiencing the evening. Aartis which will be your treat to the eye.

Timing: entry timing is 6:00 am to 10:00 pm at night.

Ticket: No entry fee required.

12. Jagdish Temple

The beautiful temple in Udaipur is a perfect icon of Hindu mythology. You must come here to enjoy its special sculptures and beautiful paintings of this place and you can see many more best places to visit in Udaipur

Timing: Temple is open 5:00 am – 2:30 pm in the morning and 4:00 pm – 10:00 pm in the evening.

Ticket: Entry is free here.

Top 5 places to visit in Udaipur

The following is a list of places you can visit through Travel agencies in Delhi, in Udaipur:

Jagdish Temple

The Jagdish Temple in Udaipur is widely regarded as one of the city’s most popular tourist destinations. The temple was constructed in the Indo-Aryan style by Maharana Jagat Singh. Temples in Udaipur have located a short distance from the City Palace, accessible by the Badi Pol entrance.

You’ll be blown away by the brass picture of a Garuda and the Vishnu deity carved from a slab of black stone. Lord Ganesha, Shiva, the Goddess Shakti, and the Sun God all have their temples. Tour operators in delhi advices, don’t forget to attend the calming aarti ceremony.

Garden of the Maidens

During the early years of the 18th century, Garden of the Maidens was built by Maharana Sangram Singh. This astounding innovation catered to the servants who accompanied the Princess of Udaipur whenever she visited her husband’s hearth.

The gorgeous marble elephants, a lotus pool, fountains, and kiosks are adequate witnesses to the wealth that existed during this era. This location also included statues of ladies singing, dancing, and enjoying.

Kumbhalgarh Fort

Kumbhalgarh fort, located approximately 64 kilometers from the city of Udaipur, is an architectural marvel and one of Rajasthan’s most lavish palaces and forts. The fort is perched on a cliff 2100 meters above sea level, protected by a massive wall stretching for 30 kilometers.

The Kumbhalgarh Fort’s design is both straightforward and breathtaking. Travel companies in India states that the fort is a popular tourist destination in Udaipur since both, the journey there and the view from the top are worth the trip.

Ahar Museum

The Ahar museum is home to some stunning and highly valuable Western Indian antiquities. If you’re interested in history, you shouldn’t miss this museum during your time in Udaipur. The magnificent structures constructed here serve as a memorial to the Maharajas of the kingdom, who was cremated in this area.

Artifacts and treasures dating back to the 15th century are housed in the inner rooms. Antiques like these are considered remnants of a long-gone kingdom and are popular attractions for visitors.

Lake Pichola

The picture-perfect lake is encircled on all sides by towering hills, opulent mansions, sacred shrines, and ghats for bathing. Thanks to the many islands serving tourist hotspots, you’re certain to have a great time on a cruise across Lake Pichola. The sunsets are breathtaking. Views of the city from the water are beautiful, especially when the sun sets and the city lights turn on.
