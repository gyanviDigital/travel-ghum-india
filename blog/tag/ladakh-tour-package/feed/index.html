]]> Why India in Monsoon is a Beauty

India in monsoon is a sight to behold. The parched lands quench their thirst, the greenery comes alive, and the air feels fresher. Monsoon transforms India’s landscape into a vibrant canvas of lush forests, blooming flowers, and flowing rivers. For those seeking a refreshing escape, weekend getaways during monsoon offer the perfect blend of adventure, serenity, and cultural experiences. Here are some of the best destinations to explore during this magical season.

Delhi, Agra, Mathura, Vrindavan

Ideal For

History buffs, culture enthusiasts, families, and spiritual seekers.

Things to Do

  • Delhi: Explore the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and Humayun’s Tomb. Take a walk in the rain-washed Lodhi Gardens and enjoy street food at Chandni Chowk.
  • Agra: Marvel at the Taj Mahal in its rain-drenched glory, visit Agra Fort, and explore the Mughal gardens.
  • Mathura and Vrindavan: Visit the Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple, take a boat ride on the Yamuna River, and experience the monsoon celebrations at the ISKCON temple.

How to Reach and Interconnectivity

Delhi, Agra, Mathura, and Vrindavan are well-connected by road and rail. The Yamuna Expressway offers a smooth drive between Delhi and Agra. Regular trains and buses run between these cities, making intercity travel convenient.

Attractions

  • Delhi: India Gate, Lotus Temple, Akshardham Temple.
  • Agra: Mehtab Bagh, Fatehpur Sikri, Itimad-ud-Daulah.
  • Mathura and Vrindavan: Banke Bihari Temple, Prem Mandir, Govardhan Hill.

Weather in Monsoon

Delhi, Agra, Mathura, and Vrindavan experience moderate to heavy rainfall, making the temperature pleasant and the surroundings lush green.

Varanasi, Ayodhya

Ideal For

Spiritual seekers, history lovers, and photographers.

Things to Do

  • Varanasi: Attend the Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat, take a boat ride on the Ganges, and explore the ancient temples and narrow lanes.
  • Ayodhya: Visit the Ram Janmabhoomi, Hanuman Garhi, and Kanak Bhawan. Enjoy the peaceful ambiance of the ghats along the Saryu River.

How to Reach and Interconnectivity

Varanasi and Ayodhya are well-connected by rail and road. Direct trains and buses are available between these cities. The nearest airport to Varanasi is Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport.

Attractions

  • Varanasi: Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Sarnath, Manikarnika Ghat.
  • Ayodhya: Ramkatha Park, Nageshwarnath Temple, Ayodhya Research Centre.

Weather in Monsoon

Both cities experience moderate rainfall, which adds a mystical charm to the ghats and temples.

Mount Abu, Udaipur, Jodhpur

Ideal For

Nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and couples.

Things to Do

  • Mount Abu: Trek to Guru Shikhar, explore the Dilwara Temples, and enjoy boating on Nakki Lake.
  • Udaipur: Visit the City Palace, take a boat ride on Lake Pichola, and stroll through Saheliyon Ki Bari.
  • Jodhpur: Explore Mehrangarh Fort, visit the Umaid Bhawan Palace, and wander through the blue streets of the old city.

How to Reach and Interconnectivity

Mount Abu, Udaipur, and Jodhpur are well-connected by road and rail. Udaipur and Jodhpur have airports with regular flights. Buses and trains frequently run between these cities.

Attractions

  • Mount Abu: Sunset Point, Achalgarh Fort, Mount Abu Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Udaipur: Jag Mandir, Fateh Sagar Lake, Bagore Ki Haveli.
  • Jodhpur: Jaswant Thada, Mandore Gardens, Rao Jodha Desert Rock Park.

Weather in Monsoon

These cities experience moderate to heavy rainfall, enhancing the beauty of lakes, forts, and palaces.

Jaipur, Pushkar

Ideal For

History buffs, culture enthusiasts, and adventure seekers.

Things to Do

  • Jaipur: Explore the Amber Fort, City Palace, and Hawa Mahal. Enjoy a rain-drenched walk through the pink city.
  • Pushkar: Visit the Brahma Temple, take a holy dip in Pushkar Lake, and explore the local markets.

How to Reach and Interconnectivity

Jaipur and Pushkar are well-connected by road and rail. Jaipur has an international airport, and regular buses and trains run between these cities.

Attractions

  • Jaipur: Jantar Mantar, Nahargarh Fort, Albert Hall Museum.
  • Pushkar: Savitri Temple, Pushkar Camel Fair (if visiting during the fair), Rose Garden.

Weather in Monsoon

Jaipur and Pushkar receive moderate rainfall, making the weather pleasant and ideal for sightseeing.

How to Reach and Interconnectivity

Gaya and Bodhgaya are well-connected by rail and road. The nearest airport is Gaya Airport. Rajgir and Nalanda can be reached by road from Gaya.

Attractions

  • Gaya and Bodhgaya: Vishnupad Temple, Dungeshwari Cave Temples.

Weather in Monsoon

These cities experience moderate rainfall, making the ancient sites and spiritual places more serene and beautiful.

Diskit Monastery

The list of tourist attractions in Ladakh cannot be complete without mentioning Diskit Monastery. The white gompa is the largest and oldest monastery in the Nubra Valley. It only takes one trip to feel enlightened since the environment is calming and restorative. Changzem Tserab Zangpo, an enthusiastic disciple of Tsong Khapa, the founder of Gelugpa, established Diskit Monastery in the 14th century. So, the Best Ladakh tour package allows you to see this monastery. 

Hemis National Park 

Hemis National Park is tucked away in the eastern Ladakh area of Jammu and Kashmir. It is the Snow Leopard Capital of India. This national park was established in 1981 and is renowned for housing the world’s highest concentration of snow leopards. The fact that Hemis is the only national park in the north of the Himalayas is another feature. Moreover, according to statistics, it is home to 16 animal and 73 bird species.

Nubra Valley

The paradise-like Nubra Valley is located 150 kilometers northeast of Leh and is one of Ladakh’s must-see destinations. Further, this valley, perched 3048 meters above sea level, has the confluence of the Shyok & Siachen Rivers, white dunes, double-humped camels, rich, verdant settlements, sparkling streams, and jagged mountain peaks. The valley has abundant scenic beauty as it rests calmly on the sides of the Karakoram mountain range. Further, explore the valley while mounted riding Bactrian camels.

Shyok River

The Shyok River is another of Leh Ladakh’s most popular tourist attractions. This Indus tributary originates in the Rimo Glacier, one of the Siachen Glacier’s forks in the Karakoram Mountains. Numerous individuals and animals perished while attempting to cross this river, earning it the nickname “The River of Death.” The sight of the river is spectacular, and it is undoubtedly one of the most beautiful spots you will see in the Leh region.

Zorawar Fort 

The Zorawar Fort honors General Zorawar Singh’s conquest of Ladakh. In 1930, he constructed this fort overlooking the slopes and the alpine city of Leh to commemorate his victory. Further, the Zorawar fort is among the top sites in Ladakh if you love incredible architecture. The building of the fort is composed of local clay, stones, sun-dried bricks, and timber frames with hinged edges.

Royal Leh Palace

Leh Palace, built in 1553 and situated atop the desolate Tsemo Hill, was formerly the royal family’s dwelling. The structure is comparable to the Potala Palace in Lhasa. Further, it is constructed of smaller stones, wood, sand, and mud. It is also famous as Lhachen Palkhar.
