Itinerary at Glance :-
Day 01:- Arrive Delhi for Incredible India tour
On arrival at Delhi international airport, after collecting luggage and clearing immigration formalities, meet and greeted by our friendly driver cum representative outside exit gate# 5, After pickup proceed towards your pre-booked hotel in Delhi on your incredible India tours by ghum india ghum, On arrival at hotel check-in and rest of the day free to relax from jetlag. Overnight in Delhi.
 

Day 02:- Delhi Local Sightseeing( Red Fort, Lotus Temple, Humayun Tomb are closed on Monday)
Today post your delicious buffet breakfast at hotel, Check-out and go for full day Delhi city tour by ghum india ghum, first go for Old Delhi city tour- by visiting Birla temple, the Raj Ghat- place is dedicated memory of Mahatma Gandhi, driving past Red fort, Then may visit Jama Masjid- India Largest Mosque are important places to visit, you may also visit Chandni Chowk, spice market etc. For a better understanding of the Old Delhi tour you may go for a Rickshaw ride tour and ride through the bazaars and streets of Old Delhi (Optional cost). Post Lunch, proceed for a city tour of New Delhi. Starts with a tour of The Qutub Minar which is a UNESCO site in Delhi and Iron pillar inside its complex. Then visit Lotus temple, which is quite similar to Sydney Opera house. It is a worship place for Bahai in Delhi. Also see Iconic India gate which is dedicated to brave Indian soldiers who lost lives during WWI and WWII, Don't forget to see huge government buildings in Delhi Like -Rashtrapati bhawan, New and Old Parliament building, Raj path. Post Delhi local tours with Ghum India Ghum drive back to the hotel. Overnight in Delhi.

Day 03:- Delhi - Agra Local Sightseeing
Post delicious breakfast at your hotel, start 3rd day of Golden Triangle India tours by driving to Mughal city Agra(220 kms/04 hrs) and on arrival check into your hotel. This afternoon visit the Agra fort or Red fort of Agra, built by the great Emperor Akbar, this was main residence of Mughal rulers till 1638 built of Red stand stones and post lunch visit most famous seven wonders of the world, Taj Mahal-the symbol of love, built by Emperor Shah Jahan which was commissioned in 1632 to house the remains of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. You may also visit the Tomb of I'timād-ud-Daulah also known as Baby Taj Mahal. Post completion of Place to visit in Agra tour or best itinerary for Agra tour, stay overnight in Agra hotel. Note:- Taj Mahal closed on Friday
 
Day 04:- Agra - Jaipur (By Surface 260 kms/ Approx. 5 hrs)
After a healthy breakfast at the hotel, Start your 4th day of Golden Triangle tour India and drive to Jaipur city popularly known as the pink city India. This is the only city in the world designed in such a way it symbolises the nine divisions of the universe through the nine rectangular sectors subdividing it as per Vedic architecture. The historic walled city of Jaipur in Rajasthan was established on the plain unlike other cities of this region which are situated on hilly terrains, It was founded in 1727 AD (18th Cent.) under the rule of Sawai Jai Singh II. Jaipur is known for its exemplary architectural legacy which is based on ancient Hindu, Mughal and western cultures. Pink city Jaipur made its official entry into the UNESCO World Heritage Site list recently in 2019. On arrival transfer to your hotel in Jaipur. Night stay in Jaipur.

Day 05:- Jaipur Local sightseeings
Post breakfast at hotel, meet your tour guide for full day Jaipur tour, start with first visiting Amber Fort (one need to reach here by 8:30-9:00 am, to avoid rush and heat) fort is situated on one end of city, Amber fort is huge is size and it has unique history, present day Amber fort was built by Raja Man Singh main attractions within Amber fort are Sheesh Mahal, Diwan-e-aam, temples and underground tunnels. Next place to see in Jaipur is Jal Mahal which is a huge palace situated in the middle of Man Sagar lake, from distance the water palace looks like its only one floor palace but in reality is four levels of this palace beneath the water. Post Lunch visit Hawa Mahal or winds palace is a architectural marvel designed by Lal Chand Ustad in 1799, The main purpose was to allow royal family ladies to see marketplace and busy streets from several jharokhas and without being seen, Then visit City palace of Jaipur which is full of architectural wonders and it is official residence of present day Royal family, it is divided into two parts one contains several courtyards along with museums and the other where present day royal family still resides. Also visit Jantar Mantar an astronomical observatory adjoining the City Palace complex, Later in evening you may visit Albert Hall and insta sensation Patrika gate, post fun filled Jaipur city tour by ghum india ghum return back to hotel and overnight in Jaipur.
 
Day 06:- Jaipur - Bikaner  city tour with desert visit (350kms/5-6hrs)
This morning post a healthy breakfast at the hotel, check out and drive to the next city Bikaner. On arrival Check-in to the hotel. In the afternoon go for a Bikaner sightseeing tour. Bikaner city is named after its great founder Rao Bikaji in 1488. But long back Bikaner city was the main trade center on the caravan routes from Africa and West Asia. Bikaner major features are rippling sands and abundant sunshine. The city is enclosed by a 7 Kms wall, which has five entry gates. Places to see in Bikaner are imposing walls and which have a 16th century fort housing old palaces, temples and mosques. Founded by Late Raja Rai Singh the fort is distinguished by its long range of 37 pavilions, a line of balconies and aerial windows of varying designs. Other important places to visit in Bikaner are Lallgarh Palace, the museum, Bhanda Sagar Jain, Deshnoke Rat temple and The Camel’s Breeding Farm.   Night stay in Bikaner. Places to visit in Bikaner.

Day 07:- Bikaner to Jaisalmer (330kms/5hrs)
Morning  post nutritious  breakfast drive to Jaisalmer- on the way induces a dramatic picture of utter magic and brilliance of the desert. The hostile terrain notwithstanding the warmth and color of people is simply overwhelming. Jaisalmer's one of the major sightseeing point is the 12th century Jaisalmer Fort and beautiful havelis which were built by wealthy merchants of Jaisalmer are yet another interesting aspect of the city, enjoy beauty of the sloppy sand dunes while you ramble your way in a camel safari. The famous Bhatti Rajput ruler Rawal Jaisal, Jaisalmer is named after him, he founded Jaisalmer in 1156. The drive passes by dry vegetation, with villages of colorfully dressed people. Huge no. of camels and camel carts, sheep and goats pass by you. On arrival in Jaisalmer, check in the Hotel. Rest the day is free for relaxing. Night stay in Jaisalmer.

Day 08:- Jaisalmer city tour​ with Gadisar lake​ and Camp Stay
Post Healthy buffet breakfast at hotel, Check-out and go for a city tour of Jaisalmer, start with the huge Jaisalmer Fort, situated over the Trikuta hill, this fort is the biggest and the most beautiful landmark of the city. The fort is mostly occupied by shops, stalls, and a number of other business concerns. It is also called Sonar Quila or the Golden fort, rising from the sand, the mega structure merges with the golden hues of the desert ambience. The Jain temple located inside the fort was built between 12th to 15th century, carrying testimony to Rajasthan's unique artistry. Do visit the havelis, the mansions built by the wealthy merchants, which are still in very good condition. The intricate craftsmanship is beyond parallel. The popular havelis like Alim-Singh-ki-Haveli and the Patwon-ki-Haveli are phenomenal for their exclusive cutwork, yellow and red stone treatment and the bracket-shaped decoration of peacocks. The rich merchants of Jaisalmer were influenced by the traditional style of the royals, constructed huge mansions (havelis) adjacent to each other in the nature of medieval culture and lavishly decorated walls and ceilings and intricately carved outdoors and interiors. Don't forget to see social media sensation in Jaisalmer which is Gadisar lake quite popular now a day, it is a location that offers opportunities for capturing unforgettable photographs., This is a manmade lake constructed by the main founder of Jaisalmer Emperor Rawal Jaisal
In the afternoon post lunch, visit the Khuri Sand Dunes camps and the traditional Rajasthani Desert Village. Hop on a camel back to view the sunset from the dunes, (Same camels to be used for Safari - getting to know the camels) as the sky is set on fire. It can be an unforgettable experience. Post completion of Place to visit in Jaisalmer or best itinerary for Jaisalmer tour, stay overnight in Jaisalmer hotel.

Day 09:- Jaisalmer/ Jodhpur Airport Drop  (290kms/  4.5hrs)
This is the toughest day on your trip because you need to pack your belonging with reminiscence of your Incredible India journey, post healthy breakfast, Check-out from your Camp in Jaisalmer and Our driver cum representative will pick you and drop to Jaisalmer/Jodhpur airport as per your return flight time, during airport transfer you can recall all your memories about India and its culture. Go back to your sweet home with a lifelong experience.
